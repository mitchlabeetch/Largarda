# French M&A Terminology

## Overview

French M&A (Mergers & Acquisitions) terminology used in the Largo M&A domain. This guide provides English translations, explanations, and context for French financial and legal terms.

## Financial Statements Terms

### Compte de Résultat (Income Statement)

| French Term                            | English Translation | Definition                                                      |
| -------------------------------------- | ------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Chiffre d'Affaires (CA)**            | Revenue / Sales     | Total revenue from sales of goods and services                  |
| **Excédent Brut d'Exploitation (EBE)** | EBITDA              | Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization |
| **Résultat d'Exploitation**            | Operating Profit    | Profit from core business operations                            |
| **Résultat Financier**                 | Financial Result    | Gains/losses from financial activities                          |
| **Résultat Exceptionnel**              | Exceptional Result  | One-time gains/losses                                           |
| **Résultat Net**                       | Net Income          | Profit after all expenses and taxes                             |
| **Marge Brute**                        | Gross Margin        | Revenue minus cost of goods sold                                |
| **Marge Nette**                        | Net Margin          | Net income as percentage of revenue                             |
| **EBE / CA**                           | EBITDA Margin       | EBITDA as percentage of revenue                                 |

### Bilan (Balance Sheet)

| French Term             | English Translation         | Definition                             |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| **Actif**               | Assets                      | Resources owned by the company         |
| **Passif**              | Liabilities & Equity        | Claims against the company's assets    |
| **Capitaux Propres**    | Shareholders' Equity        | Net worth of the company               |
| **Immobilisations**     | Fixed Assets                | Long-term assets (property, equipment) |
| **Créances Clients**    | Accounts Receivable         | Money owed by customers                |
| **Dettes Fournisseurs** | Accounts Payable            | Money owed to suppliers                |
| **Trésorerie**          | Cash & Cash Equivalents     | Liquid assets                          |
| **BFR**                 | Working Capital Requirement | Working capital needed for operations  |
| **Dettes Financières**  | Financial Debt              | Borrowed money (loans, bonds)          |
| **Fonds de Commerce**   | Goodwill                    | Intangible value of the business       |

### Tableau de Financement (Cash Flow Statement)

| French Term                             | English Translation       | Definition                                |
| --------------------------------------- | ------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| **Flux de Trésorerie Opérationnel**     | Operating Cash Flow       | Cash from core operations                 |
| **Flux de Trésorerie d'Investissement** | Investing Cash Flow       | Cash from investments                     |
| **Flux de Trésorerie de Financement**   | Financing Cash Flow       | Cash from financing activities            |
| **CAF**                                 | Cash Flow from Operations | Capacité d'Autofinancement                |

## Context and Usage Notes

### French Accounting Standards

- French companies use **PCG** (Plan Comptable Général) - Generally Accepted Accounting Principles
- Listed companies may use **IFRS** (International Financial Reporting Standards)
- French GAAP differs from US GAAP in several areas (goodwill, revenue recognition)

### Cultural Differences

- French business culture emphasizes formal relationships and written agreements
- **Protocole d'Accord** (LOI) is more common and detailed than in some markets
- **Conseil d'Administration** (Board) has strong role in French governance
- Employee representation (CE/CSE) is mandatory and influential

### Legal Framework

- French M&A governed by **Code de Commerce** (Commercial Code)
- **AMF** (Autorité des Marchés Financiers) regulates public transactions
- Employee consultation is legally required for certain transactions
- Tax implications differ significantly from other jurisdictions

### Translation Helper

```typescript
// src/common/ma/glossary/translator.ts
function translateFrenchTerm(term: string): string {
  const mapping = terminologyMap.find((t) => t.french.toLowerCase() === term.toLowerCase());
  return mapping?.english || term;
}

function getTermDefinition(term: string): string {
  const mapping = terminologyMap.find(
    (t) => t.french.toLowerCase() === term.toLowerCase() || t.english.toLowerCase() === term.toLowerCase()
  );
  return mapping?.definition || 'Term not found in glossary';
}
```
